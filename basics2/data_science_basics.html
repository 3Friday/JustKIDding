Data science: graphs, statistics and networks

After you have collected it, the data don't have to just sit there. Databases are used to get ratios, insights and ultimately to help make decisions.

We, humans, are not very good at glancing at a lot of data and drawing conclusions.

The role of the computer is to make all that data meaningful and useful. That can happen through statistics and visualization.

Data science is part programming, part statistics.

The most commonly used programming languages by data scientists are R and Python. Also they use other tools such as SQL and Excel.

The stages of working with data are:

  • Gathering
  • Processing: integrating, aggregating, filtering, sorting, cleansing and transforming data
  • Analyzing: finding correlations, averages
  • Communicating: explaining and visualizing data
